Research and Consulting:


I am a Senior Research Fellow in Economics at the University of New England (60% FTE) with a focus on rural development, production analysis, institutional design in ethical value chains and understanding the limits of human decision making in complex scenarios. I have supervised PhD students working on a range of topics including production analysis, rural development, food/nutritional analysis and more. Recent consulting work includes for the OECD (2018-19), World Bank (2020), Pew Charitable Trusts (2021) and the Australian Farm Institute (2021).

Impact:


Linking research to impact is a core focus of my work. I am co-founder of an ethical coffee supply chain company that implements innovative contracting approaches to achieve high levels of inclusivity and value enhancements for smallholder coffee farmers in eastern Uganda. In addition, I am Associate Editor of the Australian Journal of Agricultural and Resource Economics (2019-current) and Principal of an economic consulting firm (econ-lab) with a focus on resource economics, rural development and food/agricultural systems.

Apps and programming:


Using a combination of technologies I build full-stack systems that are focused on solving real-world problems. Examples of projects include:
  1. An automated supply chain disbursements and governance program (ADaPT)
  2. An early-stage online experimental economics platform
  3. A range of widgets developed showcasing a small range of web-development capabilities I can offer your project
